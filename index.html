Neurodegenerative Disease Progression

Neurodegenerative disease progression is characterized by the gradual decline in the function and survival of neurons, leading to devastating effects on both mental and physical health. This process typically involves the accumulation of abnormal proteins within the brain, such as amyloid plaques in Alzheimer's or Lewy bodies in Parkinson's, which interfere with cellular operations.

This database provides critical information on Neurodegenerative Disease Progression, including key genes and proteins involved, post-translational modifications (PTMs), cellular localization of proteins, and 3D structures.
